Scenario 01: Operation Krakatoa

Adversary intelligence in corporate-environmental conflicts

This scenario immerses you in the investigation of complex corporate ecosystems and the detection of reputational vulnerabilities in environmental conflicts.

Briefing: Operation Krakatoa

Context:

A multinational corporation with a pristine public image is accused by a local NGO of engaging in illegal mining operations that are causing irreversible environmental damage in a protected nature reserve. The corporation denies the allegations, but public pressure is mounting.

Mission:

As an intelligence team, your mission is to:

  1. Map the corporate ecosystem of the multinational, including subsidiaries, partners, and key executives.
  2. Analyze the NGO's claims and verify the evidence presented.
  3. Identify potential reputational vulnerabilities of the corporation.
  4. Assess the risk of escalation of the conflict.
  5. Propose a strategy to manage the crisis and mitigate the damage.

Learning Objectives:

  • Corporate ecosystem mapping: How to identify and analyze complex corporate structures.
  • Reputational risk analysis: How to assess and manage reputational threats.
  • Geopolitical risk analysis: How to understand the political and social context of a conflict.
  • Stakeholder analysis: How to identify and analyze the interests and influence of different actors.

Team Structure: Operation Krakatoa

This team combines corporate intelligence, environmental analysis, and geopolitical expertise.

  • Corporate Intelligence Analyst: Investigates the multinational's corporate structure, finances, and operations.
  • Environmental Analyst: Assesses the environmental impact of the alleged mining operations.
  • Geopolitical Analyst: Analyzes the political and social context of the conflict.
  • Reputational Risk Analyst: Evaluates the potential damage to the corporation's public image.
  • OSINT Analyst: Collects and analyzes open-source information on all parties involved.
